## Mechanical and Adhesive Anchors (Center Zone)

1. Adhesive anchors and dowels installed into concrete: A) Test to 200 lbs per dowel and record the results. B) Install per the manufacturer's published instructions.
2. Adhesive anchors: ASTM A-307, threaded rods with ASTM A-563 Grade A nuts and ASTM F436-1 flat washers, unless otherwise noted. Anchors designated as ASTM A-193 Grade B7 threaded rods to ASTM A-563 Grade C heavy hex nuts and ASTM F436.
3. Adhesive dowels: ASTM A-615, Grade 60 reinforcing steel.
4. All anchors shall be installed in accordance with ICC-ES report and COLA report and manufacturer's installation recommendations.
5. Unless otherwise noted, provide minimum embedment of anchors per ICC-ES report. COLA report and/or manufacturer's recommendation.
6. Prior to all drilling or coring, the contractor shall (1) verify the existing concrete or masonry thickness to prevent damage to the opposite face of concrete and masonry, (2) cut concrete/block, and (3) verify existing steel reinforcing locations by pachometer, (4) engineer to review installation.
7. If reinforcement is encountered during drilling, abandon and shift the hole location as allowed per the applicable ICC-ES report. If multiple holes are encountered by the rebar(s), or sound concrete between the dowel and the abandoned hole, fill the abandoned hole per the ICC-ES report/manufacturer's instructions. If neither of the above is noted above, the engineer will determine a new location.
8. All adhesive anchors shall be proof verified by a testing and inspection agency. Test 10% of all anchors.
9. Test anchoring no sooner than 24 hours after installation.
10. Each test (load) on any adhesive shall follow applicable load or tension on the anchor such as direct pull with a hydraulic jack, torque wrench, or calibrated spring-type tension.
11. Adhesive anchors shall be installed in concrete or grout having a minimum age of 21 days at the time of anchor installation.

## Structural Wood (Right Zone)

- Framing lumber: Douglas fir-larch (DF-L) grade marked by a recognized grading agency (WCLIB or WWPA):
  - No. 2 (DSG 4 (under)), No. 1 all others
- Beams and stringers:
  - Posts and timbers
- Glued laminated (glulam) lumber:
  - Combination 24F-V8
- Stud grade:
  - 2x4 studs: 8'-0" long
  - 2x6 studs: 10'-0" long
- Plywood: All plywood to be structural, APA rated
- Fire rating considerations: All plywood per applicable code
- Pressure treated lumber: All sills in contact with concrete or masonry, including mudsills, sill plates and sleepers shall be pressure treated per applicable standards.
- All wood in contact with concrete or ground shall be preservative treated or naturally durable.
- Treated wood shall be hot-dipped zinc-coated galvanized (ASTM A153) stainless steel or approved equal. Fastened with Simpson LSTA20 or per last row of the shear nailing schedule.
- Posts shall be responsible for bearing on steel plate, not directly on concrete (only as detailed or approved).
- Cutting, notching, or boring of structural lumber shall be limited per code.
- Glulam beams shall be installed per manufacturer's recommendations, with the camber (if any) placed upward.
- All exterior decks, balconies, etc.: blocking as required.
- Rough hardware: All wood shear walls shall have standard cut washers. Holes for bolts 1/16" larger than the nominal bolt diameter. Bolts to be of size, type, and grade as shown or specified.
- All wood stud walls to be 2x4 minimum with studs at 16" O.C. (lap sill plates = 4x all at bottom and double top plates at top).
- All doubled joists under all parallel partitions.
- All posts to be minimum 4x4 per code.
- All bolts to be tightened so that the washers are in contact with the wood.
- Re-tighten all anchors/bolts just before closing.
- All metal framing connectors, straps, ties, hangers, straps, etc. shall be as manufactured.
- Manufacturer or engineer approved IRC 2019 Section R4.4.1 & 2308.8.2 (2018 CBC).
- Moisture content of wood at time of placing shall not exceed 19 percent.
- 5/8 members include: ledgers, joists, P&L, u/s, sill, beams, headers, posts, and studs.
- Substitution of products listed below shall be submitted to GSE for review and approval prior to installation unless they can be shown as equivalent per applicable code.
  - Except joists and u/s, sill, members shall be:
    - Parallam PSL manufactured by Weyerhaeuser Grade 1.0 ICC-ESR-1387, LARR 25203
    - I-joist members shall be TJI joists manufactured by Weyerhaeuser, Grade 1.5
    - Per ICC-ESR1387, LARR 252022
  - Trus joist rim board shall be TimberStrand LSL manufactured by Weyerhaeuser, Grade 1.55E per ICC-ESR1387, LARR 25203.
