## Concrete (Center Column)

1. Concrete is dimensioned and placed as specified otherwise noted. Where reinforcing is not specifically shown or where details are not given, provide reinforcing similar to that shown for similar conditions, subject to review by the structural engineer.
2. All work of work pertaining to the concrete construction shall conform to the latest edition of ACI 318 "Building Code Requirements for Reinforced Concrete" and ACI standard of +/- 1/2" tolerance for tolerances for concrete construction and floor.
3. All structural concrete shall have a minimum compressive strength at 28 days and a max water/cement ratio as follows:

| Location | Strength | W/C | Type of Concrete |
|----------|----------|-----|-----------------|
| All Locations (V.I.D.) | **2,500 PSI** | **0.65** | Normal |
| Alternate | **3,000 PSI** | **0.45** | Moderate |

4. Concrete mixes shall be prepared with Type V Portland cement conforming to ASTM C150.
5. Normal weight concrete aggregates shall conform to ASTM C33. Light weight concrete aggregates shall conform to ASTM C330.
6. Water used in mixing concrete shall conform with ASTM C1602.
7. Thoroughly clean and roughen all hardened concrete and masonry surfaces in contact with fresh concrete. All surfaces shall be roughened to a full amplitude of 1/4".
8. Where dowels such as, but not limited to, connections, pins, or other anchors are to be placed into or through concrete members, exact contact of such members of embedded elements, and as needed to comply with OSHA & California plumbing code through such elements, and as needed to comply with DSA & California plumbing code shall be specified by or obtained by GC from designer/architect/engineer/project manager and installed per the manufacturer's recommendations and as close as practically possible around the embedded elements.
9. Non-shrink cement grout shall have a minimum 28 day compressive strength of 7000 PSI. Use quantity (area) LB/FT mass (ASTM C1107).
10. Defective concrete (voids, rock pockets, cracks) shall be repaired and reinforced as directed by the structural engineer.

## Reinforcement (Center Column, Lower)

1. All reinforcing bars shall conform to ASTM A-615, Grade 60, unless noted otherwise.
2. All smooth dowels: ASTM A36 KS
3. Rebar shall have the following concrete cover set as per code:

| Exposure | Cover |
|----------|-------|
| Poured against earth | 3" |
| Concrete exposed to weather (4&5 and larger) | 2" |
| Concrete exposed to weather (#4 and smaller) | 1-1/2" |
| Not exposed to weather (slabs, walls) | 3/4" |
| Other concrete not exposed to weather | 1-1/2" |

4. Bars shall be firmly supported and accurately placed as required by the ACI standards. Using tie and support bars as noted in Appendix A. Design strength, where necessary for firm and accurate placing, provide dowels to match all requirements at poor, unless specifically noted otherwise. All dowels and lap splices at the base of columns shall be maintained as indicated.
5. All principal rebar shall terminate with a standard hook minimum unless specifically detailed otherwise. Rebar detail shall be made cold. Rebar shall not be offset with any portion of the bar is engaged in concrete.
6. All lap splices are Class B lap splices unless noted otherwise.
