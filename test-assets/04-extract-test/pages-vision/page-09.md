## Mechanical and Adhesive Anchors (Center Column)

1. Adhesive anchors and dowels installed into concrete: A. Test 10% (of drilled) across the entire structure. B. Per HY 200 or shall be COLA manufacturer's standard.
2. Adhesive anchors: ASTM A-325 threaded rods with ASTM A 563 Grade A nuts and ASTM F-436-3 flat washers, unless otherwise noted. Anchors designated as ASTM A-193 Grade B7 threaded rods (or ASTM A-325 Grade A rods) to be and with Grade 8 nuts and heavy flat washers.
3. Adhesive dowels: ASTM A-615 Grade 60 reinforcing steel.
4. All anchors shall be installed in accordance with ICC-ES report and COLA report and manufacturer's recommendations.
5. Unless otherwise noted, provide minimum embedment of anchors per ICC-ES report. Comply with manufacturer's installation recommendations.
6. Prior to all drilling or coring, the contractor shall (1) verify the existing concrete or masonry thickness to prevent damage to the opposite face of concrete and reinforcing; (2) cut cores (slots, etc.); and (3) obtain written approval from structural engineer for location of reinforcement, prior to installation.
7. If reinforcement is encountered during drilling, abandon and shift the hole location as allowed per manufacturer's recommendations. Prior to grouting, remove all dust, water, oil, loose or sound concrete between the dowel and the abandoned hole, fill the void with epoxy or approved concrete patching material. If an excessive number of holes as noted above, the engineer will determine a new location.
8. All anchors shall be proof tested by direct tensile testing and inspection testing. Test 10% of all anchors.
9. Test anchors no sooner than 24 hours after installation.
10. Each test load or non-representative sample shall experience the tension on the anchor such as direct pull with a hydraulic jack, torque wrench, or calibrated spring-loaded device = 1.107.
11. Adhesive anchors shall be installed in concrete or grout having a minimum (cured 28 days at the time of anchor installation).

## Structural Wood (Right Column)

### Framing Lumber
- All Douglas fir-larch (DFL) lumber marked by a recognized grading agency (WCLIB & WWPA):
  - No. 2 (2x6 & under), No. 1 all others
  - Beams and Stringers: Posts and Timbers
  - Select structural (4x4 and larger)
  - Stud lumber/utility (2x4) for cripple walls
  - Combination 2x4 V.F.

### Stud Sizes
- 2x4 studs: 8'-0" long
- 2x6 studs: varies

### Pressure-Treated Wood
- All sill plates and wood in contact with concrete or masonry, wood within 6" of exposed soil
- Treated to a minimum retention of CCA-C or ACQ-D, AWPA U1
- Pressure-treated wood shall be incised treatment unless Southern Pine
- Fasteners in contact with preservative-treated wood shall be hot-dipped galvanized, stainless steel, silicon bronze, or copper

### Plywood and Sheathing
- All plywood for concrete formwork or exposed to weather: Exterior grade APA
- Rated sheathing per structural plans
- Cutting, notching, or boring of studs, joists, and rafters only as detailed or approved
- Posts shall be continuous without splicing

### Hardware and Connectors
- All bolted connections in wood shall have standard cut washers. Holes for bolts 1/16" larger than the nominal bolt diameter (bolt exceeds 1/2")
- All wood studs shall be 2x (min) minimum along with standard cap, tie, and floor plates
- Not less than 3x as wide (3-1/2 inches min on each side)
- Use double joists under all parallel partitions
- All fasteners and hardware: just before closing, prior to pour inspection
- Provide hardware per the manufacturer's requirements, straps, etc., shall be as manufactured
- Plywood/wood sheathing per engineer approved 2019 Section 4.4.1 & 2308.8.2 of 2019 CBC
- Moisture content of wood at time of placing shall not exceed 19 percent

### Engineered Wood Products
- All members including joists, PSL, LVL, LSL, beams, headers, posts, and studs
- Substitution of products listed below shall be submitted to GSE for review and approval prior to installation. In no case shall any products be used without approval in accordance with the manufacturer's recommendations.
- Except joists and LVL/LSL members shall be:
  - Parallam PSL manufactured by Weyerhaeuser Grade 1.8 (ICC-ESR-1387, LARR 25020)
  - Joists: members shall be TJI joists manufactured by Weyerhaeuser
  - TimberStrand LSL manufactured by Weyerhaeuser
  - LVL shall be manufactured/tested by Weyerhaeuser (grade 1.9)
  - ICC-ESR1387, LARR 25202

### Simpson Strong-Tie
- All hardware/connectors to conform to the following: Simpson/USP frames (STD), LARR25719 Simpson Strong Walls, LARR 25725
