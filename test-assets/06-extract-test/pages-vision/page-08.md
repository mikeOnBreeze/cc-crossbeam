## Stairway Guard Railings Notes (Left Side, Below Sections)
1. A guard shall be located along open-sided walking surfaces, mezzanines, stairways, ramps, and landings that are more than 30 inches above the floor or grade below. (CBC 1015.)
2. Stairway guards shall not be less than 34 inches in height measured vertically from the stair nosing. Guards for porches, balconies, and raised floor surfaces shall have a height of 36 inches to 38 inches above the leading edge of the tread. (CBC 1015.)
3. Guards at porches, balconies or raised floor surfaces shall have a height of 36 inches to 38 inches.
4. Open guards shall not permit 4-3/8 inches diameter sphere to pass through any opening. (CBC 1015.)
5. Open guards at the side of a stairway shall not have openings larger than 4-3/8 inches. Top and bottom rail shall not permit a 6 inch diameter sphere to pass through. (CBC 1015.5)

5. 6-1/4 inches maximum depth of handrail. (CBC 1012.4) (Example: 2x6, no guard combination)
6. (CBC 1012.6)
7. Handrails: 12 inches horizontally extension beyond the top riser and continue to slope for the depth of one tread width beyond the bottom riser. ## Detail 7 — Wall Assembly Penetration Detail (Bottom-Center)
U.L. System No. 604 — Scale: N.T.S.
Notes regarding fire-rated wall penetrations:
1. All rigid conduits, ducts, plumbing pipes, and appliances located within the building construction of walls of resilient silicone, mortar, or a minimum 1/4 inches (6.35mm) thick cut to fit. For the building construction of walls of resilient silicone, mortar, or fill void with approved material.
2. All approved permanent and self-closing fire dampers required for ducts.
3. Firestopping materials shall meet the test requirements of ASTM E814 and be installed to restrict the passage of flame and products of combustion.
4. Penetrations shall be protected. Metal piping and conduit are not to be installed in openings of gypsum board panels. Wall and floor/ceiling penetrations of a specific fire-resistance-rated assembly should be firestopped per listing.
5. Electrical outlet boxes on opposite sides of fire-rated walls shall be separated by a horizontal distance of at least 24 inches.
6. All penetrations of fire-rated walls should be sealed with approved listed firestop materials.
7. Metal, ventilating, and conditioning air ducts located in rated assemblies shall be lined (exception: ducting serving only exit water kitchen exhaust facilities and bathroom facilities). Ducts must not be combustible materials.
